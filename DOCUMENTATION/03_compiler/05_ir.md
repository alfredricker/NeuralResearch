# IR (Intermediate Representation)

## Purpose
IR converts syntax-shaped AST into execution-shaped graph data structures.

## IR layers

### 1) Declarative IR
Captures semantic graph declarations while preserving high-level topology intent.

- Main types:
  - `ModuleIr`
  - `GraphIr`
  - `NodeGroupIr`
  - `GroupLinkIr`
  - `InterfaceIr`
  - `ExternalLinkIr`
  - `TopologyExprIr`
- Responsibilities:
  - resolve names (`omega`, `MNIST`, `numbers`) into stable IDs
  - validate links and references
  - keep topologies symbolic (`Sparse`, `Identity`, `WeightedSum`, etc.)

### 2) Executable IR
Materializes runtime-ready nodes, edges, scheduling, and storage.

- Main types:
  - `ExecutableModule`
  - `ExecutableGraph`
  - `ExecNode`
  - `ExecEdge`
  - `StoragePlan`
- Responsibilities:
  - expand group-level declarations into per-node/per-edge structures
  - convert topology expressions into concrete edge sets
  - assign storage slots for node state

## Why two IR layers
- Declarative IR is best for correctness and diagnostics.
- Executable IR is best for simulation/runtime/codegen backends.
- Splitting these concerns keeps future optimizations and backends clean.

## Current status
- `Program -> ModuleIr -> ExecutableModule` lowering is implemented.
- Minimal graph example compiles through both IR layers successfully.
